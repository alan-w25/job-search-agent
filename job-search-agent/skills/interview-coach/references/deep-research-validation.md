# Deep Research Validation Loop

Multi-cycle research methodology that ensures company research and interview
prep are evidence-based, not surface-level. Single-pass research misses
contradictions, red flags, and non-obvious insights.

## The 7-Step Loop

```
1. HYPOTHESISE → Form initial view based on JD and known info
2. SEARCH (Primary) → Execute initial web searches
3. VALIDATE + GAP ANALYSIS → Cross-reference, identify what's MISSING
4. SEARCH (Secondary) → Target gaps and deeper questions
5. CHALLENGE + INVERT → Search for COUNTER-EVIDENCE and red flags
6. REFINE → Update assessment with full evidence
7. SYNTHESISE → Consolidate with confidence levels and caveats
```

## Step 3 is the Critical Step

After primary research, ask explicitly:
- What questions remain UNANSWERED?
- What perspectives are MISSING? (Found only positive OR only negative?)
- What claims lack sufficient evidence?
- What would make this company look WORSE than research suggests?
- Have we identified the likely HIRING MANAGER?
- Have we found RED FLAGS?

For each gap: define a secondary search query to fill it.

## Step 5: The Red Flag Hunt

Actively search for evidence AGAINST your emerging view:
- "[company]" layoffs / lawsuit / controversy
- "[company]" "toxic culture" / burnout / turnover
- "[company]" Glassdoor cons / "would not recommend"
- "[company]" executive departures / resigned / departed

Document both what you found AND what you searched for but didn't find.
"Searched but not found" is itself a positive signal worth reporting.

## Source Credibility Hierarchy

| Source Type | Weight | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| SEC/regulatory filings | Highest | Legally required to be accurate |
| Major news outlets | High | Editorial standards, but may lag |
| Company official sources | Medium-High | Self-serving but factual |
| LinkedIn verified profiles | Medium | For people intel |
| Glassdoor (20+ reviews pattern) | Medium | Individual reviews low; patterns medium |
| Blog posts / social media | Low | Useful for culture signals, low factual weight |

## Citation Requirements

Every factual claim must include:
- Source name
- URL (when available)
- Access date
- Credibility assessment (High/Medium/Low)

## Quality Gate

Before presenting research, verify:
- [ ] Multiple sources consulted per finding
- [ ] Both positive AND negative evidence sought
- [ ] Gaps explicitly identified and addressed
- [ ] Red flag hunt completed
- [ ] All claims cited with sources
- [ ] Confidence level documented
- [ ] Remaining uncertainties acknowledged
