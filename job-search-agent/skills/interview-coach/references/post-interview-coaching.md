# Post-Interview Coaching & Rejection Diagnosis

Diagnose what went wrong, categorize the gap, extract actionable lessons, and
build a concrete path forward. Empathetic but not indulgent.

## Stage-Specific Diagnosis

Where rejection occurred determines what was actually being tested:

| Stage | What Was Assessed | Rejection Likely Means |
|-------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Application / ATS | CV keywords, basic qualifications | CV/ATS mismatch, keyword gaps, volume competition |
| Recruiter screen | Logistics, salary, communication, story clarity | Positioning, expectation mismatch, articulation |
| Hiring manager | Role fit, experience depth, chemistry | Experience framing, relevance, rapport |
| Technical round | Actual capability demonstration | Genuine skill gap OR preparation gap |
| Final round | Culture fit, team dynamics, stakeholder buy-in | Fit/timing, competing candidates, internal factors |
| Post-offer | Compensation, references, business factors | Negotiation, references, budget freeze |

**Critical rule:** Match the depth of analysis to the depth of the interview.
- Don't recommend upskilling after a recruiter screen (skills weren't assessed)
- Don't minimize final round rejections (they invested significantly)

## Reconstruct What Happened

Guide factual reconstruction, not interpretation:

**For recruiter screens:** How long was the call? Did salary come up? How did
you answer "tell me about yourself"? Was it agency or internal?

**For hiring manager:** What questions were asked? List them. How did you answer
each? What was the energy like? Did they sell you on the role or just assess?

**For technical:** What was the format? Where did you handle well? Where did
you struggle? Any real-time feedback?

**For final rounds:** Who did you meet? What themes repeated? Any awkward
moments? Did they discuss next steps enthusiastically?

## Gap Categorization

Based on stage and reconstruction, diagnose the gap type:

### Skill Gap
**What it is:** Missing a core capability the role required.
**Indicators:** Couldn't answer technical questions. Got stuck on a coding
problem. Didn't know a framework they use.
**Fix:** Training, projects, certifications. Takes weeks to months.
**Action:** Identify the specific skill. Find the fastest path to working
knowledge. Update target role criteria if the gap is large.

### Signal Gap
**What it is:** You HAVE the skills but failed to communicate them.
**Indicators:** Strong background but vague answers. Said "we" instead
of "I". Buried the result. Didn't quantify impact. Rambled.
**Fix:** Interview practice, STAR story refinement. Takes days to weeks.
**Action:** Identify which stories fell flat. Rewrite them with the
result first, specific actions second. Practice out loud.

### Fit / Timing Gap
**What it is:** Right person, wrong moment or wrong match.
**Indicators:** Good interview energy but "went with someone with more X
experience." Internal candidate. Budget cut. Reorg.
**Fix:** Usually can't be fixed — it's external.
**Action:** Don't internalize this. Extract what you learned about what
companies at this stage need. Adjust targeting.

## Evidence Anchoring

After diagnosis, anchor the user in evidence of their value:

"What's still true" exercise: Pull 3-5 concrete achievements from their
profile that are undeniably strong. The rejection doesn't erase these.
Frame as: "You still built X that served Y users. You still led Z that
saved $N. One company's pass doesn't change the evidence."

## Pattern Tracking

If multiple rejections:
- Map each to a gap type (skill / signal / fit)
- Look for patterns: Are they all signal gaps? → Practice problem, not skills
- Are they all at the same stage? → Something specific to that stage needs work
- Are they across stages? → Likely a targeting problem
- Track in the application tracker in the career profile

## Post-Interview Deliverables

Generate:
1. **Diagnosis summary**: Stage, gap type, evidence, likely cause
2. **Lessons learned**: What to do differently next time (specific, not generic)
3. **Story updates**: If signal gap, rewrite the weak STAR stories
4. **Follow-up actions**: Thank-you email, ask for feedback, adjust strategy
5. **Updated tracker**: Record in the application tracker

## Thank-You Email Generation

When the user wants a follow-up email after any interview (not just rejection):
- Reference a specific moment from the conversation (ask for one)
- Reinforce their strongest answer
- If they fumbled a question, include a brief "I've been thinking more
  about X and wanted to share..." recovery
- Warm but not desperate. Professional but not robotic.
- Keep it under 150 words.

## Tone Calibration

- No toxic positivity: "their loss" and "everything happens for a reason" are banned
- Normalize rejection with data: 6-10 rejections average before an offer
- Calibrate emotional support to rejection stage (phone screen ≠ final round)
- Be honest about uncertainty — sometimes we genuinely can't know why
- Separate facts from the stories they're telling themselves about the facts
