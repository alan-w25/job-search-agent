# Strategic Questions Framework

Questions the user should ask in interviews, organized by purpose. Each question
serves dual duty: it gathers intel AND signals something positive about the candidate.

## Role Clarity Questions

These show you care about doing the job well, not just getting the job.

- "What would a successful first 90 days look like in this role?"
  → Intel: reveals actual priorities vs. JD aspirations
  → Signals: you think about ramp-up and impact

- "What's the biggest challenge the person in this role will face in the next 6 months?"
  → Intel: surfaces the real problems they need solved
  → Signals: you're thinking about obstacles, not just the happy path

- "How does this role contribute to the team's / company's goals this year?"
  → Intel: reveals if the role is strategic or an afterthought
  → Signals: you care about context and impact, not just tasks

- "Is this a new position or a backfill? If backfill, what happened?"
  → Intel: massive — were they promoted (good), fired (neutral), or did they quit (why?)
  → Signals: you do your due diligence

## Team & Culture Questions

- "Can you walk me through a typical week on the team?"
  → Intel: meetings vs. focus time, collaboration vs. independent work
  → Signals: you're practical, not just theoretical

- "How does the team handle disagreements about technical direction?"
  → Intel: culture of debate vs. hierarchy
  → Signals: you're not afraid of conflict, you want to know how it's handled

- "What's the team's approach to on-call / incident response?"
  → Intel: burnout risk, operational maturity
  → Signals: you're aware of real-world operational needs

- "How would you describe the working relationship between engineering and [product/design/data]?"
  → Intel: cross-functional health
  → Signals: you care about collaboration

## Growth & Development

- "What does career growth look like for someone in this role?"
  → Intel: promotion criteria, growth ceiling
  → Signals: ambition without seeming like a flight risk

- "Is there a budget for learning, conferences, or certifications?"
  → Intel: investment in employees
  → Signals: growth mindset

- "Who are the most successful people in similar roles here, and what makes them successful?"
  → Intel: the unwritten success criteria
  → Signals: you study what excellence looks like

## Strategic / Impressive Questions

These show you think at a higher level than the role requires.

- "I noticed [Company] recently [launched X / acquired Y / raised Z]. How does that
  affect this team's priorities?"
  → Intel: shows how leadership decisions cascade
  → Signals: you've done your homework AND you think about strategy

- "What's the team's biggest technical debt, and is there appetite to address it?"
  → Intel: engineering maturity, prioritization philosophy
  → Signals: you understand real engineering environments

- "If I asked your team what they wish leadership understood better about their work,
  what would they say?"
  → Intel: disconnect between leadership and ICs
  → Signals: empathy, emotional intelligence

## Red Flag Probing Questions

Polite ways to test concerns without sounding negative.

- "How would you describe the pace here? I've seen it described as fast-paced —
  what does that look like day-to-day?"
  → Tests if "fast-paced" means "exciting growth" or "constant fire-fighting"

- "What's the typical tenure on this team?"
  → Low tenure = retention problem

- "How does the company approach work-life balance, especially during crunch periods?"
  → Evasive answer = there is no balance

- "Has the scope of this role changed since it was first posted?"
  → Scope creep before you even start = trouble

## Questions to NEVER Ask in Early Rounds

- Salary / benefits (wait until they bring it up or you're in offer stage)
- Vacation days (signals you're already thinking about time off)
- "What does the company do?" (shows zero research)
- Anything easily found on their website
- "Did I get the job?" (comes across as insecure)

## Timing Rules

- **Phone Screen**: 2-3 questions, keep it focused on role and process
- **Hiring Manager**: 3-4 questions, mix of role clarity and team/culture
- **Technical Round**: 1-2 questions, focused on technical practices and challenges
- **Final Round / Executive**: 2-3 strategic questions that show big-picture thinking
