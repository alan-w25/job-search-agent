# Outreach Templates

Proven templates for every networking scenario. Each template is a starting point —
you MUST customize with specific details about the recipient. A template sent
without personalization is spam.

Golden rule: every message should answer three questions for the recipient:
1. Who is this person?
2. Why are they reaching out to ME specifically?
3. What do they want?

---

## LinkedIn Connection Requests

LinkedIn connection requests have a 300-character limit. Every character counts.
Do NOT waste space on filler ("I hope this message finds you well").

### Template 1: Alumni Connection

```
Hi {{NAME}} — fellow {{SCHOOL}} alum here ({{GRADUATION_YEAR_OR_PROGRAM}}).
I'm a {{YOUR_ROLE}} exploring {{TARGET_AREA}} and noticed you're doing great
work at {{COMPANY}}. Would love to connect!
```
Character count target: 200-280

### Template 2: Same Company / Former Colleague Network

```
Hi {{NAME}} — I see you're at {{COMPANY}}! I spent {{DURATION}} at
{{SHARED_COMPANY}} on the {{TEAM}} team. Really interested in what
{{COMPANY}} is building in {{AREA}}. Would love to connect.
```
Character count target: 200-280

### Template 3: Same Industry / Shared Expertise

```
Hi {{NAME}} — I've been following your work on {{SPECIFIC_TOPIC}}. I'm a
{{YOUR_ROLE}} focused on {{OVERLAPPING_AREA}} and your {{POST/TALK/PROJECT}}
on {{TOPIC}} really resonated. Would love to connect.
```
Character count target: 200-280

### Template 4: Mutual Connection

```
Hi {{NAME}} — {{MUTUAL_NAME}} suggested I reach out. I'm a {{YOUR_ROLE}}
interested in {{AREA}} at {{COMPANY}}, and they thought we'd have a lot
to talk about. Would love to connect!
```
Character count target: 200-280

### Template 5: Cold (No Shared Context)

```
Hi {{NAME}} — I came across your {{POST/TALK/ARTICLE}} on {{TOPIC}} and
found it really insightful, especially {{SPECIFIC_DETAIL}}. I'm a
{{YOUR_ROLE}} working in {{AREA}}. Would love to connect and learn more.
```
Character count target: 200-280

### Anti-Patterns for Connection Requests

- FAIL: "I'd like to add you to my professional network on LinkedIn." (the default — never use it)
- FAIL: "Hi, I'm looking for a job at your company. Can you help?" (too transactional, too early)
- FAIL: No message at all (accept rate drops by 40%+ without a note)
- FAIL: "Dear Sir/Madam" or any overly formal salutation (this is LinkedIn, not a legal brief)
- FAIL: Listing your entire background in the request (save it for after they connect)
- FAIL: "I see we have X mutual connections" as the only personalization (low effort, everyone can see that)

## Coffee Chat / Informational Interview Requests

These go via LinkedIn message (after connecting) or email. Keep under 150 words for
initial outreach. Longer messages have measurably lower response rates.

### Template 1: Warm (Existing Connection or Strong Shared Background)

Subject (if email): Quick question about {{AREA}} at {{COMPANY}}

```
Hi {{NAME}},

Thanks for connecting! I wanted to reach out because I'm exploring
opportunities in {{TARGET_AREA}} and your experience at {{COMPANY}} really
stands out — especially {{SPECIFIC_THING_ABOUT_THEIR_WORK}}.

I'm currently a {{YOUR_ROLE}} at {{YOUR_COMPANY}}, where I've been focused
on {{RELEVANT_WORK}}. I'm looking to move into {{TARGET_DIRECTION}} and
would love to hear your perspective on {{SPECIFIC_QUESTION_OR_TOPIC}}.

Would you be open to a 15-20 minute call in the next couple of weeks? I'm
flexible on timing and happy to work around your schedule.

Either way, I appreciate you connecting — {{REFERENCE_TO_THEIR_RECENT_WORK}}.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Template 2: Moderate (2nd-Degree or Shared Background)

Subject (if email): Fellow {{SHARED_CONTEXT}} — quick question about {{COMPANY}}

```
Hi {{NAME}},

I came across your profile through {{HOW_YOU_FOUND_THEM}} and was really
impressed by {{SPECIFIC_ACCOMPLISHMENT_OR_DETAIL}}.

I'm a {{YOUR_ROLE}} with {{X}} years in {{DOMAIN}}, and I'm exploring a
move into {{TARGET_AREA}}. I noticed you made a similar transition from
{{THEIR_PREVIOUS_CONTEXT}} to {{THEIR_CURRENT_CONTEXT}}, and I'd love to
hear how you navigated that.

I have 2-3 specific questions about {{TOPIC}} — would you have 15 minutes
for a quick call? Happy to send my questions in advance so you can decide
if it's a good use of your time.

Thanks for considering,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Template 3: Cold (No Shared Context — Strategic Outreach)

Subject (if email): Your {{TALK/POST/PROJECT}} on {{TOPIC}} — quick question

```
Hi {{NAME}},

I recently {{READ_YOUR_ARTICLE / WATCHED_YOUR_TALK / SAW_YOUR_PROJECT}} on
{{SPECIFIC_TOPIC}} and it directly relates to a challenge I've been working
on in {{YOUR_AREA}}.

Specifically, {{ONE_SENTENCE_ABOUT_WHAT_RESONATED_AND_WHY}}.

I'm a {{YOUR_ROLE}} at {{YOUR_COMPANY}} focused on {{RELEVANT_DOMAIN}}.
I'd love to ask you 2-3 questions about {{SPECIFIC_TOPIC}} if you have a
few minutes — even a quick email exchange would be incredibly helpful.

I know cold outreach is a lot, so no pressure at all. Either way, thanks
for putting that {{CONTENT}} out there.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Anti-Patterns for Coffee Chat Requests

- FAIL: "I'd love to pick your brain" (vague, overused, implies you want free consulting)
- FAIL: "Can we grab coffee?" to someone in a different city (shows you didn't read their profile)
- FAIL: No specific reason you're reaching out to THEM (feels like mass outreach)
- FAIL: "I'm passionate about..." without specifics (empty filler)
- FAIL: Asking for 30-60 minutes in the first message (too much commitment from a stranger)
- FAIL: Sending a wall of text (respect their inbox — keep it scannable)
- FAIL: "I know you're busy, but..." (everyone says this — it doesn't help)

## Follow-Up Templates

### After Connection Accepted (LinkedIn)

Send within 24-48 hours of acceptance. This is your chance to start a real
conversation — do NOT waste it on a referral ask.

```
Hi {{NAME}} — thanks for connecting!

I noticed {{SPECIFIC_OBSERVATION — their recent post, a project they shared,
a role transition they made}}. {{ONE_SENTENCE_CONNECTING_IT_TO_YOUR_INTERESTS}}.

I'm currently {{BRIEF_CONTEXT — 1 sentence max}}. Would love to hear more
about your work at {{COMPANY}} sometime if you're open to it.

Thanks again,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### After Informational Interview / Coffee Chat

Send within 24 hours. Reference specific things from the conversation to show
you were actually listening.

```
Hi {{NAME}},

Thank you so much for taking the time to chat today. I really valued your
perspective on {{SPECIFIC_TOPIC_DISCUSSED}} — especially your point about
{{SPECIFIC_INSIGHT_THEY_SHARED}}.

I've already started looking into {{SOMETHING_THEY_RECOMMENDED — a resource,
a company, an approach}} and it's exactly what I needed.

I'll keep you posted on how things develop with {{RELEVANT_NEXT_STEP}}.
And if there's ever anything I can help with on my end — {{SPECIFIC_OFFER
BASED_ON_YOUR_SKILLS}} — don't hesitate to reach out.

Thanks again,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### After No Response (First Follow-Up)

Wait 5-7 business days. Send exactly ONE follow-up. If no response after this,
move on.

```
Hi {{NAME}},

I wanted to follow up on my earlier message — I know things get buried in
the inbox. I'm still very interested in hearing your perspective on
{{SPECIFIC_TOPIC}}.

If now isn't a good time, I completely understand. I'd be happy to connect
later if that's easier.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Anti-Patterns for Follow-Ups

- FAIL: Following up the next day (too aggressive — wait 5-7 business days)
- FAIL: "Just circling back..." or "Just bumping this..." (passive-aggressive tone)
- FAIL: Multiple follow-ups after no response (one follow-up is the maximum — after that, silence is an answer)
- FAIL: "Did you see my message?" (yes, they saw it)
- FAIL: Making the follow-up longer than the original message (keep it shorter)
- FAIL: Adding guilt ("I was really hoping to hear back")

## Referral Request Templates

Only use these after at least one meaningful interaction. Sending a referral request
to someone you've never spoken to is a FAIL — it puts them in an awkward position
and virtually guarantees a no.

### Template 1: Close Contact (Former Manager, Close Colleague, Mentor)

```
Hi {{NAME}},

Hope you're doing well! I wanted to reach out because I saw {{COMPANY}} has
an opening for a {{ROLE}} that looks like a great fit for my background —
especially given my work on {{RELEVANT_PROJECT_THEY_KNOW_ABOUT}}.

Here's the posting: {{LINK}}

Since you know my work from {{SHARED_CONTEXT}}, would you be comfortable
submitting a referral? I've attached my updated resume and here are a few
points that might be useful:

- {{BULLET_1 — quantified achievement relevant to the role}}
- {{BULLET_2 — relevant skill or experience}}
- {{BULLET_3 — culture/team fit angle}}

Totally understand if the timing or situation doesn't work. Either way, let's
catch up soon — it's been too long.

Thanks,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Template 2: Acquaintance (Had 1-2 Good Conversations)

```
Hi {{NAME}},

Thanks again for our conversation about {{TOPIC}} — your insight on
{{SPECIFIC_POINT}} has really shaped how I'm approaching my search.

I noticed {{COMPANY}} has an opening for {{ROLE}} ({{LINK}}), and based
on what you shared about the team, it seems like a strong fit for my
background in {{RELEVANT_AREA}}.

Would you be open to submitting a referral? I want to make it as easy as
possible, so I've attached my resume and included a few bullet points you
could use:

- {{BULLET_1}}
- {{BULLET_2}}
- {{BULLET_3}}

No pressure at all — I completely understand if this isn't something you're
comfortable with. Our conversation was valuable regardless, and I appreciate
the time you've given me.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Template 3: Recent Connection (Met Once, Had an Informational Interview)

```
Hi {{NAME}},

I've been thinking a lot about our conversation on {{DATE/CONTEXT}} —
particularly what you said about {{SPECIFIC_TOPIC}}. It confirmed that
{{COMPANY}} is a place where I could make a real impact.

I noticed the {{ROLE}} opening ({{LINK}}) and wanted to ask: would you
feel comfortable passing along my information to the hiring team? I know
we've only connected recently, so I want to be upfront — if you'd rather
not, I 100% understand and it won't affect how much I valued our chat.

If you are open to it, here's what might be helpful to share:
- {{BULLET_1}}
- {{BULLET_2}}
- {{BULLET_3}}

My resume is attached. And either way — {{REFERENCE_TO_SOMETHING_THEY
CARE_ABOUT_OR_FOLLOW_UP_ON_CONVERSATION}}.

Thanks,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### Anti-Patterns for Referral Requests

- FAIL: Asking in the first interaction (always too early, no matter how well it went)
- FAIL: Being vague about the role ("anything that might be a fit")
- FAIL: Not providing bullet points or your resume (you're making them do all the work)
- FAIL: Pressuring or guilt-tripping ("I really need this")
- FAIL: Asking multiple people at the same company simultaneously without telling them
  (they may find out and it looks bad)
- FAIL: Not giving an easy out (they should feel comfortable saying no)
- FAIL: Sending a referral request via LinkedIn connection request message (wrong channel, too early)

## Thank-You Templates

### After Informational Interview

(See Follow-Up After Informational Interview above — that IS the thank-you.)

### After Receiving a Referral

```
Hi {{NAME}},

I just wanted to say a huge thank you for submitting the referral for the
{{ROLE}} at {{COMPANY}}. I know it takes effort and I really appreciate
you putting your name behind mine.

I'll keep you posted on how it goes. And regardless of the outcome, I owe
you one — if there's ever anything I can do for you, please don't hesitate
to ask.

Thanks again,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

### After They Helped but It Didn't Work Out

```
Hi {{NAME}},

I wanted to let you know that the {{ROLE}} at {{COMPANY}} didn't work out
this time — but I wanted to thank you again for the referral/introduction.
It meant a lot that you were willing to vouch for me.

I'm continuing my search and focusing on {{DIRECTION}}. If you hear of
anything in that space, I'd love to know — but no pressure at all.

I hope we can stay in touch regardless. {{GENUINE_WELL_WISH_OR_REFERENCE
TO_THEIR_WORK}}.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
```

## "Keeping Warm" Templates

These are the messages you send when there's no specific ask — just maintaining
the relationship. They are the most underused and highest-ROI networking activity.

### Share a Relevant Article

```
Hi {{NAME}} — saw this article on {{TOPIC}} and immediately thought of our
conversation about {{RELATED_TOPIC}}. Thought you might find it interesting:
{{LINK}}

Hope things are going well at {{COMPANY}}!
```

### Congratulate on Achievement

```
Hi {{NAME}} — congratulations on {{PROMOTION / WORK_ANNIVERSARY / PUBLISHED
ARTICLE / SUCCESSFUL_LAUNCH}}! Really well-deserved. {{OPTIONAL: ONE
SENTENCE_ABOUT_WHY_IT_RESONATED}}.
```

### Share Your Own Update

```
Hi {{NAME}} — wanted to share a quick update since we last spoke. I
{{RELEVANT_UPDATE — started a new role, completed a project, published
something, learned something new}}. Your advice about {{THING_THEY
ADVISED}} really helped shape that direction.

Hope you're doing well — would love to hear what you've been up to.
```

### Anti-Patterns for Keeping Warm

- FAIL: Only reaching out when you need something (people notice this pattern fast)
- FAIL: Generic "Happy holidays!" messages with no personal touch
- FAIL: "Checking in" with nothing of value to share (feels like a sales cadence)
- FAIL: Over-frequency — monthly is the maximum for most professional contacts
- FAIL: Under-frequency — if you only reach out once a year, you're a stranger

---

## General Anti-Patterns (Apply to All Messages)

These will sink ANY outreach regardless of template:

- FAIL: Copy-pasting the same message to multiple people (they will compare notes)
- FAIL: Using someone's full formal name ("Dear Mr. Jonathan Smith" on LinkedIn)
- FAIL: Typos, especially in names or company names (instant credibility killer)
- FAIL: Being self-deprecating ("I know I'm not qualified, but...")
- FAIL: Being arrogant ("I would be a perfect fit for your team")
- FAIL: Writing in third person ("{{YOUR_NAME}} is a seasoned professional...")
- FAIL: Including a full resume or CV in the body of a message
- FAIL: Using LinkedIn InMail when a connection request would suffice
- FAIL: Sending messages at odd hours and expecting immediate responses
- FAIL: Any form of dishonesty about your intent, experience, or connection to them
